1. Product Overview
PayPaddy's The Lagos Standard is a collateralized escrow marketplace — Nigeria's first &quot;Hardened Trust Protocol.&quot; The platform enables buyers to pay securely via CBN-licensed bank escrow, while sellers stake real collateral as a trust signal. Trinity Verification (BVN + NIN + Liveness) ensures every participant is identity-verified.
The current state: wireframes complete, no live product. This PRD specifies the full MVP.
1.1 Vision Statement
Eliminate the trust tax in Nigerian e-commerce — making online transactions safer than in-person ones.

2. User Personas
2.1 Primary: The Cautious Buyer
Profile: Urban professional aged 25-40, Lagos. Tech-savvy, has been scammed before (or knows someone who has). Currently drives to Computer Village for high-value purchases despite time cost.
Goals: Buy electronics safely online. Save time and avoid in-person risk.
Pain Points: No accountability for sellers. No refunds. Fake reviews. Payment reversals.
Trigger Moment: Needs a laptop/phone costing ₦200K+. Dreads the Computer Village trip.
2.2 Secondary: The Established Merchant
Profile: Electronics trader, 10-15 years in business, Computer Village, Ikeja. Strong offline reputation. Wants to expand online but lacks trust signals.
Goals: Grow online sales channel. Protect hard-earned reputation. Get paid reliably.
Pain Points: Chargebacks. No way to prove legitimacy online. Complex e-commerce platforms.
Trigger Moment: Loses a sale because the buyer does not trust online ordering.
2.3 Tertiary: The Emerging Seller
Profile: Part-time electronics reseller, 22-30, sells on WhatsApp/Instagram. Building a business.
Goals: Build reputation from scratch. Attract buyers who do not know them.
Pain Points: Zero trust signals. Frequent scam accusations. Payment delays.

4. Solution: The Three Pillars
Pillar 1 — Trinity Verification
BVN (financial identity via NIBSS) + NIN (government ID via NIMC) + Liveness/SIM age check. Everyone verifies. No shortcuts.
Pillar 2 — Collateral Staking
Sellers stake ₦100K+ (Silver) or ₦500K+ (Gold). Fraud = collateral slashed. Economic skin-in-the-game.
Pillar 3 — Escrow + SafeGuard
Payments held in CBN-licensed bank escrow. 2% of all fees → SafeGuard Pool (network insurance).

11. Future Roadmap: Version 2.0+
The following features represent strategic extensions beyond the initial four-phase roadmap. They are informed by the Trust Protocol research and competitive analysis, and are designed to deepen PayPaddy’s moat as Africa’s trust infrastructure layer for e-commerce.
Phase 5A — Web of Trust / Vouching System
New users cannot transact until a high-reputation user vouches for them. If the vouched user commits fraud, the voucher loses reputation or staked capital. This creates a social trust layer where existing trusted users have skin-in-the-game when onboarding newcomers — think of it as a referral system where your reputation is the collateral.
Phase 5B — Decentralized Arbitration (Juror Model)
Randomly select 3–5 high-reputation users as blind-voting jurors for disputes. Jurors who vote with the majority earn rewards; those who vote incoherently lose reputation. This is far more scalable than platform-mediated disputes and distributes the cost of conflict resolution across the community.
Phase 5C — Offline Cash Agent Network
Recruit local convenience stores as “Human ATMs” who stake capital to process cash withdrawals. This bridges PayPaddy’s digital escrow system to the physical cash economy — critical in a market where a large portion of transactions still involve cash.
Phase 5D — Micro-Hedging Against Naira Volatility
Hold user balances in USD-pegged stablecoins and convert to naira only at the point of withdrawal. This protects users from inflation and currency devaluation during the escrow holding period — a significant trust differentiator in a high-inflation environment.
Phase 6 — Trust-as-a-Service API (“Visa of Trust”)
Open the escrow and trust verification system as a public API so other marketplaces, logistics platforms, and fintech companies can plug into PayPaddy’s trust infrastructure. This transforms PayPaddy from a marketplace into a platform — the “Visa of Trust” for African digital commerce.
Recommended Licensing &amp; Integration Partners
Escrow Banking: Providus Bank, Wema Bank, Sterling Bank. Biometric Verification: Smile ID (liveness detection). KYC Providers: Prembly &amp; Youverify. NIN Verification: NIMC NINAuth. Payment Processing: Paystack. Note: PSSP license requires ₦250M capital base + ₦100M security deposit with CBN.
Competitive Positioning &amp; Moat Strategy
PayPaddy is the only platform combining biometric verification + seller collateral + escrow + SafeGuard pool. Key competitors include Jumia and Konga (general e-commerce), Vesicash and AtaraPay (escrow specialists), and Paystack/Flutterwave (payment infrastructure). Six defensive moats protect this position: (1) Innovator’s Dilemma Shield — incumbents won’t copy collateral staking as it alienates their existing sellers; (2) Trust Data Moat — verified transaction histories cannot be exported; (3) SafeGuard Pool actuarial advantage — requires data that only accumulates over time; (4) Network effects from Web of Trust vouching; (5) Regulatory moat from being first with CBN-compliant escrow; (6) Brand trust as “the safe option” for Nigerian e-commerce.
